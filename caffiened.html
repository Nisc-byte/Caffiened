Processing of Our Coffee Beans

Ripening

Coffee cherries are hand-picked when they reach optimal ripeness, ensuring the best flavor potential.

Harvesting

The harvested cherries are sorted to remove any underripe or overripe fruit, maintaining quality control.

Drying

The sorted cherries are spread out in thin layers to dry under the sun, reducing moisture content.

Hulling

Once dried, the outer layers of the cherries are removed to extract the green coffee beans inside.

Roasting

The green beans are roasted to develop their flavor profiles, with roasting levels tailored to each variety.

Processing

After roasting, the beans are cooled, packaged, and prepared for distribution to ensure freshness.

Packaging

The final step involves packaging the roasted beans in airtight bags to preserve their aroma and flavor until they reach the consumer.
